Market higlights

NITROGEN

Urea prices ended 2016 on a strong note and in some places at their highest level of the year. While the Christmas and New Year holiday slowed activity, the few sales that took place all saw increased prices and the outlook was optimistic for January. Granular urea supply was tight in Asia. Chinese exports shrunk to 330-340,000t/month in October and November and are unlikely to rise in Q1 2017. For calendar 2016, it is likely that Chinese urea exports fell by more than 5mn tonnes compared to 2015, doing much to bring supply and demand back into equilibrium. In the West, Brazilian buying switched off at the end of December, purchasing for the safrinha season coming to an end and attention became fully focused on the US. Granular urea prices moved up again in the US Gulf and were equivalent to $265/t cfr for February arrival, lagging spot prices in the AG by $5-10/t. Reduced supply of prilled urea from Ukraine has tightened supply in the Black Sea and has obliged Turkish buyers to look at alternative sources. Iranian, Egyptian and, possibly, Baltic urea will move to cover requirements for January. Despite the current firm market, looking at average prices for the year as a whole, it is evident that 2016 was a disappointing time for the urea industry. Fob prices in the Arabian Gulf, Egypt and the US Gulf fell by about $80/t compared to 2015, averaging $206/t fob, $216/t fob and $206/st fob respectively. 2016 was the fourth successive year of falling prices:

EGYPT

A couple of additional sales took place the fourth week of December. OCI sold 30,000t of granular urea for December shipment to the US at $273/t cfr. Freight from Damietta to Nola for 30,000t was put at $18-19/t, suggesting a netback in the mid- $250s/t fob. Prior to this, Mopco sold 10,000t of granular urea to a trader at $255/t fob for January shipment, which was followed by a 15,000t sale on 22 December at $261/t fob, also for January shipment. Egyptian producers were still trying to reach agreement with the ministry of agriculture on a formula under which urea would be priced for domestic deliveries January-April. In the interim, producers were to start delivering some urea to the local market from the last week of December. While in principle the government could take up to 55pc of production, it was expected output from the country’s seven export-oriented urea plants would be split roughly one-third for the domestic market and two-thirds for export. This should permit exports of 200-220,000t/month in January.

ALGERIA

Ameropa was in the freight market for 30,000t of granular urea to load in Arzew prompt for the US Gulf. Freight ideas were around $19/t. It loaded one 30,000t cargo from AOA for Spain in December and another for the US. AOA invited bids for granular urea for January loading by close of business on 24 December for award on 25 December. Sorfert loaded about 35,000t of granular urea in December but was now concentrating on domestic sales, selling about 1,500t/day into the local market and reported better prices than for export. It sold for export in earlier December based on a formula price of Egypt fob + $6/t.

NIGERIA

Trammo bought 30,000t of granular urea from Indorama at a price in the low-$240s/t fob for mid-January loading. Prior to this, Keytrade was to load 30,000t of granular urea for Brazil 28-30 December. Indorama was not offering at present because it might have to close its Port Harcourt plant for 30 days from mid-January for turnaround.

BENIN

The Association Interprofessionnelle de Coton (AIC) issued a tender, closing on 5 January, for the following products: Urea – 40,000t NPK 14-18-18+6S+1B – 100,000t NPK 13-17-17+6S+5B+1.5Zn - 25,000t

PHOSPHATE

Liquidity was scarce but prices stable-to-firm in a month dominated by announcements from Mosaic which were set to impact the global phosphates market. In Europe, Moroccan and Russian producers were seeking to increase DAP prices but there were no new sales. In Latin America, sellers were attempting to hold prices as Eurochem reported new sales into Brazil. The US market continued its mild resurgence in the fourth week of December with Mosaic reporting DAP sales at $305/st fob Nola but the heavy import line-up in January looked set to prevent prices from rising too high. The consensus from traders was that international prices were bottoming out as India remained quiet despite the subsidy rollover. American producer Mosaic confirmed its much-rumoured acquisition of Brazil-based Vale’s fertilizer assets for $2.5bn as it continues to expand into South America. The deal involves the purchase of phosphate rock mines in Brazil, as well as chemical and fertilizer manufacturing plants with a nameplate capacity of 4.8mn t/yr of finished phosphates. Meanwhile, Acron confirmed its plans to diversify into MAP and DAP production- which is expected to reach 600,000t – 900,000t/yr capacity. Both these announcements highlight producers’ preference towards vertical integration and diversification on the supply-side. The Vale deal is Mosaic’s latest shift away from its domestic market following the 2013 announcement of its 25pc stake in the $7bn Wa’ad Al Shamal joint-venture with Sabic and Ma’aden. On the subcontinent, Indian demand was suppressed by increasingly high Chinese offers and DAP stocks of over 1mn t. Deepak scrapped its tender as prices were deemed too expensive- reportedly at $330/t fob ex-China; the Blue Deebaj and IPL demand was yet to be met as Chinese producers refuse to give way on prices. Stocks fell almost 400,000t over the month but remained high at just above 1mn t, offering Indian buyers some slack in the face of tight Chinese supply. In China, operating rates were reported to be below 60pc nationwide following the consensus reached amongst producers in the third week of December at the Guiyang meeting. Seasonal domestic demand was still lacklustre but offer prices rose. This means producers had little interest in exporting DAP/MAP. Producers were offering DAP at $320-330/t fob to the international market but had been achieving sales of as high as $340/t fob equivalent in the domestic market. As home demand was expected to remain strong until March, a price reduction is unlikely in 1Q 2017 even if there is a cut in export duty.

MOROCCO

OCP made no new sales since the third week of December with discussions ongoing for January. As with Russian producers, OCP was pushing for a $10-15/t increase on DAP prices into Europe for January loading. January availability remains tight given that two more NPS vessels were expected to load for Ethiopia (100,000t) and a rolling turnaround was taking place. TUNISIA

GCT was running at 60pc of capacity on DAP. It reported the sale of 10,000t DAP to Romania in the mid-$330s/t fob for early January shipment. December saw the producer still loading the balance of DAP for Gubretas/Turkey under the current contract. Some additional trucks were moving to Libya and GCT said around 10,000t would move domestically. End- December was to see a TSP vessel also loading for Bangladesh under formula. There was a freight inquiry to load 25,000t TSP Sfax to Chittagong/Mongla 23-31 December. There were no TSP sales to European destinations.

KENYA

Awarding of the Kenyan DAP tender remained opaque with some suppliers reportedly receiving notification of orders for NP/NPK and DAP for the tender but not the final contracts. No volume had been specified. It was unclear whether other potential suppliers also received these notifications.




POTASH

The market remained steady in the fourth week of December, leaving participants in no doubt that the period since August has been the most stable of the year. Few sales were reported in the last week in December, although indications were that suppliers are bullish heading into 2017, and will seek to capitalise on the price momentum over the past few months. Suppliers were targeting $245-250/t cfr for granular MOP in Brazil, and €245-255/t cfr for granular MOP in Europe from January. And North American producers raised offers to $265/st fot Corn Belt in the US. But the $240/t cfr offers for standard MOP in southeast Asia did not appear to be rising at this stage. Buyers will resist the increases, particularly in some western European countries that suffered from poor crop yields and harvests. Expectations are for a quiet start to 2017. The market awaits the start of Chinese consortium negotiations, expected soon after Chinese New Year. But 2017 will see the start-up of the 1.4mn t/yr Turkmenkhimiya mine in Turkmenistan, the 2.86mn t/yr Legacy mine and an extra 900,000 t/yr from a third site at Esterhazy — both in Saskatchewan, Canada — as well as Eurochem’s 3.7mn t/yr Usolskiy and 4.6mn t/yr Volgakaliy projects in Russia. With so much new capacity coming onstream, it is difficult to see how prices will remain stable in a market that is already oversupplied.

MOROCCO

BPC said it had sold 30,000t of standard MOP to OCP at a rate of $220/t cfr, although this was not confirmed with the buyer. A 30,000t vessel was due to load on 20-22 December, headed for Jorf Lasfar.
